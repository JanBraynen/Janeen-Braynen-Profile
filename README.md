## About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher in the Ware Lab at Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory, where I investigate how sorghum adapts to nutrient stress to improve crop resilience, productivity, and sustainability.

My research integrates transcriptomics, single-cell genomics, ionomics, and phenotypic analyses to uncover the molecular mechanisms underlying nutrient adaptation, with a particular focus on iron (Fe) and nitrogen (N) stress responses.

I am passionate about translating discoveries in plant biology into strategies that support sustainable agriculture and global food security.


## Research Interests

* Crop Improvement

## Current Research

I am currently investigating the molecular mechanisms underlying nutrient adaptation in sorghum. My research integrates genomics, transcriptomics, ionomics, and phenotypic analyses to better understand how plants respond to nutrient stress and to identify traits that may improve crop resilience and productivity.

Carbon Partitioning NAM (CP-NAM) Sorghum Population

Research Focus: Investigating natural variation in nutrient adaptation among diverse sorghum founder lines.


## Nutrient Adaptation in Forage Sorghum

Research Focus: Understanding how forage sorghum responds to nutrient stress at the cellular and whole-plant levels.
